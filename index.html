Your Destinations

Hanoi

Vietnam's capital is a city of contrasts — ancient temples sit beside French colonial architecture, and buzzing motorbike streets open onto peaceful lakes. The Old Quarter is a sensory experience of narrow lanes packed with street food vendors, silk shops, and coffee houses. November brings mild, comfortable weather perfect for exploring on foot.

Known For: Pho (rich bone broth with fresh herbs and rice noodles), bun cha (grilled pork with vermicelli, made famous by Obama's visit), egg coffee (ca phe trung — creamy whipped egg yolk over strong Vietnamese coffee), and banh mi (crispy baguette sandwiches with pate, pickled vegetables, and herbs). Hanoi's food is subtle, aromatic, and built on layered broths.

Lan Ha Bay / Ha Long Bay

A UNESCO World Heritage landscape of thousands of limestone karsts and islands rising from emerald waters. Cruising through the bay feels otherworldly — expect dramatic cliff faces, hidden caves, floating fishing villages, and stunning sunrises. The Genesis Regal cruise navigates the less-crowded Lan Ha Bay area for a more intimate, boutique experience.

Known For: Fresh seafood prepared onboard — whole grilled prawns, steamed fish with ginger and lime, crab spring rolls, and Vietnamese-style seafood hot pot. The cruise sources directly from local fishing villages. Expect full-board fine dining plus cooking classes where you can learn to make spring rolls and pho.

Da Nang & Hoi An

Da Nang is a modern coastal city with long stretches of white-sand beach, luxury resorts, and a relaxed pace. Just 30 minutes away, Hoi An Ancient Town is a UNESCO site known for its lantern-lined streets, centuries-old architecture, and world-famous tailor shops where you can get custom clothing made in 24–48 hours.

Known For: Cao lau (thick noodles with pork, herbs, and crispy croutons — only made in Hoi An), banh xeo (sizzling crispy crepes stuffed with shrimp and bean sprouts), mi quang (turmeric noodles), and white rose dumplings (delicate shrimp dumplings unique to Hoi An). The food is colorful, fresh, and deeply regional.

Ho Chi Minh City (Saigon)

Vietnam's largest city is energetic, modern, and full of nightlife. District 1 is the heart of the action — rooftop bars, Michelin-starred restaurants, designer boutiques, and bustling markets like Ben Thanh. This is where the trip reaches its glamorous finale with the All Black Everything evening.

Known For: Bold southern-style Vietnamese food — broken rice with grilled pork (com tam), fresh spring rolls (goi cuon), hu tieu (southern pork noodle soup), and banh trang tron (rice paper salad snack). Anan Saigon (your farewell dinner) holds a Michelin star for reimagining street food into elevated plates. Expect sweeter flavors, more coconut milk, and tropical fruit.

Vietnam Visa & Entry Requirements

  • Most U.S. travelers need a visa to enter Vietnam
  • Estimated single-entry e-visa cost: approximately $25 USD
  • This itinerary should only require single entry
  • Begin reviewing requirements 90–120 days before departure
  • Submit the e-visa application at least 8 weeks before departure (by late September 2027)
  • Passport must be valid for at least 6 months beyond arrival with blank pages available
  • Print and save digital copies of passport, visa, flights, hotel confirmations, and travel insurance
Do not wait until the last minute to apply for the Vietnam e-visa. Travelers without required documents may be denied boarding or entry.

Helpful Tips & Know Before You Go

Apps to Download

  • Grab — rideshare and transportation
  • Google Translate
  • Google Maps with offline maps downloaded
  • WhatsApp for group communication
  • Airline apps

Money

  • Vietnam uses the Vietnamese Dong (VND)
  • Credit cards accepted at hotels and larger restaurants
  • Carry cash for markets, street vendors, tips, and small businesses
  • Notify banks and credit card companies before travel

Transportation

  • Use Grab whenever possible
  • Share ride details if traveling separately
  • Travel together at night
  • Hotel-arranged transfers for airport and group movements

Group Travel Expectations

  • Be on time for scheduled activities
  • Respect different vacation styles and personal time
  • Communicate if plans change or if running late
  • Stay flexible
  • Have fun, take pictures, and celebrate Keysha's 40th birthday in Vietnam!

Budget & Tipping Tips

  • Meals at nice restaurants will cost around $10–$20 USD per person; street food and casual spots are $3–$7
  • All included meals are listed, but we encourage you to tip tour guides, drivers, and cruise staff
  • Please be mindful that our tour guides and drivers work largely off TIPS — it is customary and appreciated
  • Shopping will be available at each location — please make sure you have separate money for food, shopping, and tips
  • Please tell your banks/credit card companies that you will be traveling abroad
  • As with any tourist destination, pickpocketing and theft can be a concern in busy areas. Be vigilant and don't leave belongings unattended. Use a money belt or hidden pouch for passports, credit cards, and cash. Avoid flashing valuable items.
  • Food & Stomach Prep: Vietnamese food is fresh and delicious, but your stomach may need time to adjust to new ingredients, spices, and street food. Bring probiotics (start taking them a week before the trip), Imodium, and motion sickness/nausea medicine (Dramamine or Bonine — helpful for the cruise and winding roads). Stay hydrated and only drink bottled or filtered water.
